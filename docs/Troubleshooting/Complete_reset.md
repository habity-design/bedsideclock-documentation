# Fix Missing Sounds or Silent Audio

If your clock's sound list is empty or no audio plays, a *complete reset* followed by a firmware update will fix the issue. The latest firmware includes stability fixes that prevent it from happening again.

**Before you start:** The reset clears the current time, and the firmware update erases your saved alarm times. Make a note of your alarms so you can set them up again afterward.

## Step 1: Fully drain the battery

The reset only happens when the battery is completely flat. Unplug your clock and leave it unplugged for at least 3 days.

**Note:** The display will show "Low battery" before the battery is fully drained. This message alone does not mean the battery is flat - you still need to wait the full 3 days.

## Step 2: Reconnect power

Connect the clock to power. If it doesn't power on by itself, short-press the reboot button next to the USB-C port.

## Step 3: Confirm the reset worked

Check the time on the display:

- **Time shows 00:00** - the battery was fully drained and the reset was successful. Continue to the next step.
- **Time shows the correct time** - the battery wasn't fully drained. Unplug the clock and wait another day before trying again.

## Step 4: Test the sounds

Open any sound list and play a sound to confirm audio is working again.

## Step 5: Update the firmware

Keep the clock connected to power and update it via Wi-Fi. See our [update guide](/More/How_to_update) for instructions. This step is important - the update prevents the issue from coming back.

## Still not working?

If your clock is still silent after completing all the steps, try the full process one more time. If the issue persists, contact support.
